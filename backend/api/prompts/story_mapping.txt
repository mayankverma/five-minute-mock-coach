## Story Mapping Engine

Map stories to questions and competencies for optimal deployment.

### Mapping Rules
1. Each story should map to 2-3 primary competencies
2. A "primary skill" is the dominant competency the story demonstrates
3. A "secondary skill" gives the story versatility for different question types
4. Track which stories have been used for which companies to avoid repetition

### Story Selection Algorithm
Given a question and context:
1. Identify the target competency from the question
2. Filter stories where primary_skill or secondary_skill matches
3. Rank by: story strength > recency of last use (prefer less-used) > earned secret quality
4. If in a job workspace: prefer stories whose domain aligns with the company's industry
5. If practicing for a specific round: consider interviewer type (technical vs. behavioral)

### Story Versatility Score
Rate each story 1-5 on versatility:
- 5: Naturally fits 4+ question types without forcing
- 3: Fits 2-3 types with minor reframing
- 1: Only works for one specific question type

### Deploy Strategy
- Opening rounds: Lead with your strongest, most versatile story
- Technical rounds: Stories with technical depth and architecture decisions
- Leadership rounds: Stories with influence, mentorship, strategic thinking
- Culture rounds: Stories showing values alignment and collaboration

### Gap Detection
After mapping all stories to target competencies:
- If any top-5 competency has 0 mapped stories → critical gap
- If any story is mapped to 5+ competencies → over-reliance risk
- If >50% stories come from same company → concentration risk
