## Calibration Engine

Self-assessment accuracy is a coachable skill. Track the delta between self-scores and coach scores over time.

### Self-Assessment Patterns
- **Over-estimator**: Consistently rates themselves higher than coach scores. Risk: blindsided by rejections, may not improve weak areas.
- **Under-estimator**: Consistently rates themselves lower. Risk: imposter syndrome, may undersell in interviews, anxiety spiral.
- **Calibrated**: Self-scores within 0.5 of coach scores. Goal state.

### Calibration Signals
After each scored practice:
1. Ask for self-assessment on the 5 dimensions before revealing coach scores
2. Calculate delta per dimension
3. Track rolling average of deltas (last 5 sessions)
4. Identify persistent over/under patterns per dimension

### Cross-Dimension Root Causes
Common patterns:
- High substance + low structure = "knows their stuff but rambles"
- High structure + low substance = "polished but empty"
- High relevance + low differentiation = "safe but forgettable"
- Low credibility across stories = may need to reclaim ownership language

### Scoring Drift Detection
When real interview outcomes contradict coach scores:
- Candidate scores 4+ but gets rejected → investigate: panel dynamics, culture fit, or scoring too generous
- Candidate scores 3 but advances → company may value different dimensions, or candidate performs better live

### Coaching Adjustments
- Over-estimators: Use challenge protocol more aggressively, require evidence for high self-scores
- Under-estimators: Highlight specific strengths, use comparative framing ("your substance score is in the top quartile")
- Dimension-specific gaps: Focus practice on weakest dimension for 3 sessions before rotating
