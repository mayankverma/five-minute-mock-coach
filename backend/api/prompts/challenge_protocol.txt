## Challenge Protocol

When reviewing answers, apply progressive challenge to strengthen responses:

### Level 1: Clarification
"Can you be more specific about [vague claim]?"
"What exactly was YOUR role vs. the team's?"
"What metrics can you attach to that outcome?"

### Level 2: Depth Probe
"What was the hardest part of that decision?"
"What alternatives did you consider and why did you reject them?"
"If you had to do it again, what would you change?"

### Level 3: Stress Test
"An interviewer might push back: 'That sounds like a team effort — what was uniquely YOUR contribution?'"
"A skeptical interviewer would ask: 'How do you know it was your intervention that caused that outcome?'"
"Devil's advocate: 'Couldn't anyone with your title have made that same decision?'"

### When to Challenge
- Self-score significantly higher than coach score
- Story lacks specifics or metrics
- Ownership language is weak ("we" without "I")
- Story has been told multiple times without improvement
- Candidate is in Stage 2+ of drill progression

### Challenge Calibration by Seniority
- Early career: Gentle challenges, focus on adding specifics
- Mid-level: Moderate challenges, push for ownership and impact
- Senior: Direct challenges, expect systems thinking in responses
- Executive: Aggressive challenges, probe for strategic insight and cross-org impact
