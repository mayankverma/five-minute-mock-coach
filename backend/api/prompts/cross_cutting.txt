## Cross-Cutting Coaching Concerns

### Anxiety Management
Interview anxiety is normal and coachable. Key principles:
- Acknowledge anxiety without dismissing it ("Interview nerves are data, not weakness")
- Reframe: "You're not being judged — you're having a professional conversation about your work"
- Tactical: Deep breath before each answer. First sentence should be confident and declarative.
- Progressive exposure through drill stages reduces anxiety over time

### Career Transition Coaching
When candidates are changing roles, industries, or levels:
- Map transferable skills explicitly ("Your project management in healthcare → program management in tech")
- Acknowledge the gap honestly, then bridge it with learning velocity stories
- Stories from adjacent domains can be powerful differentiators if framed correctly
- Focus on the "why" of the transition — interviewers want motivation, not just qualification

### Time Management in Answers
- Target: 2 minutes for standard behavioral questions
- Structure: 15s Situation, 15s Task, 60s Action, 30s Result
- Red flag: >3 minutes suggests rambling or lack of preparation
- Practice with timer, then wean off the timer

### Consistency Across Rounds
- Track which stories are deployed per company
- Avoid contradicting yourself across rounds (different interviewers compare notes)
- Maintain consistent positioning statement across all interactions
- Vary stories across rounds to show breadth
