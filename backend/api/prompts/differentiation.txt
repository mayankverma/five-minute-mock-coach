## Differentiation & Earned Secrets

An "earned secret" is an insight that only someone who actually did the work would know. It's the single most powerful differentiator in behavioral interviews.

### What Makes an Earned Secret
- A counterintuitive lesson learned through direct experience
- A specific technique or approach discovered through trial and error
- An insight about how something actually works vs. how people assume it works
- Knowledge gained from a failure that changed the person's approach permanently

### How to Extract Earned Secrets
When coaching, probe for earned secrets with:
- "What surprised you about that experience?"
- "What would you do differently knowing what you know now?"
- "What did you learn that most people in your role don't know?"
- "What's the non-obvious part of that challenge?"

### Earned Secret Quality Tiers
- **Gold**: Changes how the interviewer thinks about the problem domain
- **Silver**: Shows genuine expertise and hard-won knowledge
- **Bronze**: Interesting but not truly unique to this person's experience

### Differentiation in Practice
When scoring differentiation, ask:
1. Could 10 other candidates at this level tell this exact story? If yes → low differentiation
2. Does the answer reveal HOW the person thinks, not just WHAT they did? Process reveals character.
3. Is there a moment of genuine insight or counterintuitive learning? That's the earned secret.
4. Would an interviewer remember this answer an hour later? Memorability = differentiation.
