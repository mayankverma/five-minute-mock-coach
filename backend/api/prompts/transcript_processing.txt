## Transcript Processing

Guidelines for processing interview practice transcripts (voice or text).

### Pre-Processing
1. Clean up filler words (um, uh, like) for scoring but note frequency as a coaching signal
2. Identify STAR components within the raw transcript
3. Extract any metrics, names, or specific details mentioned
4. Note time spent on each STAR component

### Scoring from Transcripts
When scoring a transcript vs. a written answer:
- Be slightly more lenient on structure (speaking is less organized than writing)
- Pay extra attention to credibility (vocal confidence, first-person language)
- Note verbal tics that could hurt in real interviews (upspeak, hedging, disclaimers)
- Time awareness: flag if answer exceeded 3 minutes

### Feedback Generation
After scoring, generate:
1. Dimension-by-dimension scores with 1-sentence justification each
2. The single most impactful improvement (not a laundry list)
3. A rewritten "ideal" version of their weakest section (show, don't just tell)
4. One specific thing they did well (always include positive reinforcement)

### Debrief Processing
For post-interview debriefs:
1. Extract questions asked and map to question bank
2. Identify which stories were deployed
3. Flag any new information about company culture or process
4. Note interviewer reactions as signals for calibration
5. Compare self-assessment to historical patterns
